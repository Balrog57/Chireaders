⚡ Bolt: O(1) read chapters lookup

💡 What:
Replaced `isChapterRead(url, chapter.url)` inside `renderChapter` and long press handlers with a locally memoized `Set` inside `NovelDetailScreen`. Checks are now `readChaptersSet.has(chapter.url)`.

🎯 Why:
The previous implementation destructured the `isChapterRead` helper from context, which performs an `Array.some()` inside. Because this was called inside a `.map` over the entire chapters list during rendering, the time complexity became O(N*M), where N is the number of chapters in the list and M is the number of read chapters for that novel. This caused noticeable lag when opening novels with many read chapters.

📊 Impact:
Reduces chapter read-status lookups from O(N*M) to O(1). This completely eliminates list render stutter when viewing novels with hundreds of chapters and a large read history.

🔬 Measurement:
Open a novel with >1000 chapters and >500 marked as read. The novel detail screen will load immediately, and opening/closing chapter buckets or sorting will happen instantaneously. Verified by running local format checks (`pnpm lint`) and localized syntax checks (`tsc`).
