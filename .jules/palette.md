## 2024-05-19 - Adding Screen Reader Context to Series Cards

**Learning:** When navigating lists of novels (like in `HomeScreen`, `LibraryScreen`, or `HistoryScreen`), the `TouchableOpacity` wrappers around the series cards and history items were completely opaque to screen readers because they lacked `accessibilityRole`, `accessibilityLabel`, and `accessibilityHint`. This meant users relying on assistive technologies would only hear the raw text inside (if any) without context that the item was actionable or where it would take them.
**Action:** Always ensure that interactive structural elements, especially cards in `FlatList` or `ScrollView`, explicitly define `accessibilityRole="button"`, provide a descriptive `accessibilityLabel` (like the series title and read date), and include an `accessibilityHint` to clarify the resulting action (e.g., "Ouvrir les détails de la série").
